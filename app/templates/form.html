What is this?

This webapp explores protein sonification- that is, the translation of protein sequences to music. First, we determine a key signature and tempo based off of the first 10 proteins in the sequence. The proteins are divided into two groups of 5 and the unicode point of each protein character is found. Sums of both groups are taken and hashed to a specific key and tempo. Keys included are C major, D major, G major, D minor, and G minor. Tempos are 60, 125, 200 and 300.
We then iterate through the protein sequence and assign a note to each individual amino acid. The number of notes in the song is equal to the total number of acids in the sequence. The duration of the note is determined based on the overall distribution of amino acids in a given protein sequence. We also differentiate between conserved domains and unconserved domains. Sections of the protein which are conserved are represented with chords (and are colored teal within the sequence), while other sections of the protein are comprised of single notes.
